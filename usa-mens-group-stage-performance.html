How the USA has performed against expectations in the Men's World Cup

Every United States men's World Cup group, by Elo strength of schedule and performance · 1930–2026

USA won 2 games in the Group Stage for the first time since the inaugural 1930 Men's World Cup. It's a fantastic result, but they were also gifted the benefit of being the host with a top seed, so let's evaluate how they performed against the expectations. First, strength of schedule: how strong were the opponents the day the tournament kicked off (their average Elo rating)? Second, performance: how many group points did the USA actually win against that schedule, versus the points an Elo model expected, and what Elo level did the team effectively play at?

By eloratings.net's numbers the draw was about average: the opponents came in at 1841 Elo, #8 of 12, well short of the 2014 “Group of Death” (1906). The catch is where the USA sat among them. Rated 1726, it was the weakest team in its own group on paper (Turkiye 1911, Paraguay 1834, Australia 1777). It won its first two, then rested starters and lost the finale 2–3 to Turkiye, but still topped the group at 2-0-1. Six points against an expected 3.9 is +2.1, the second-biggest over-performance of any USA group on record. The results put the team at the level of an 1895-rated side, well above its own 1726.

How the numbers were built

Elo ratings are the official figures published by eloratings.net, taken from each tournament's “World Cup start” table, so every opponent is rated as it stood the day that World Cup kicked off (no hindsight). eloratings.net uses standard Elo with a 100-point home advantage and a goal-difference multiplier, with K weighted by importance (60 for World Cup finals down to 20 for friendlies); see their method. At the 2026 start its five strongest sides are Spain 2157, Argentina 2115, France 2063, England 2024, Brazil 1991. Expected points come from pairing those ratings with a Poisson-scoreline model that respects each game's venue (home edge in 1994 and 2026, the away game at Italy in 1934, neutral sites otherwise); the performance rating is the Elo level at which the USA's results would have been expected. USA match results are from the martj42 international dataset; group labels and scorelines were verified against Wikipedia. The 2026 finale (USA 2–3 Turkiye) is final; the USA finished 2-0-1 and won the group.
